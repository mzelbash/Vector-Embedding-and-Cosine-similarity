1 Dot Product and Angle: The Core Idea

The dot product of two vectors measures how much they point in the same direction. For unit vectors (length = 1), the dot product equals the cosine of the angle between them. Rotate vector B with the slider and watch what happens.

Cosine Similarity Formula
cos(θ) = A·B / (|A| × |B|)
For unit vectors: cos(θ) = A·B

3 Discovering the Plurality Direction

Word vectors encode grammar too. Subtracting dog from dogs extracts a "plurality direction" in the embedding space. Any word that aligns with this direction has a higher plurality score. Watch how one, two, three, four rank differently.

Higher score = more aligned with the plural direction.
What we see: dog and dogs point in the same general direction but are not identical. The shift from dog to dogs is small but consistent across all singular/plural pairs (cat/cats, city/cities, etc.).
The key operation:
plurality = dogs − dog

The result is a direction vector. It points "from singular to plural." This direction is consistent across many word pairs in the embedding space.
Ranking by plurality: We measure how much each number word aligns with the plurality direction using cosine similarity. Words associated with larger counts score higher because they embed more "plural" context.
Vector arithmetic:
dogs  = [0.82, 0.62]
dog   = [0.82, 0.22]
dogs − dog = [0.00, 0.40]
normalized  = [0.00, 1.00]
The plurality direction points straight up in this 2D space.
Summary What Cosine Similarity Captures
↑↑
cos ≈ +1
Same direction. Words are closely related in meaning.
↗→
cos ≈ 0
Perpendicular. Unrelated concepts with no shared context.
↑↓
cos ≈ -1
Opposite direction. Words appear in contrasting contexts.
Direction encodes meaning. Arithmetic on directions encodes relationships.
dogs − dog ≈ cats − cat ≈ cities − city  (the same plurality shift)
